## SECTION 8: Care & Feeding (280 words)

**Content Type:** Explanation

### H2 Heading:
"Haltung und Fütterung: Hardy und anspruchslos wie die Karst-Vorfahren"

1. **Herkunft der Robustheit: Die Karst-Adaption** (85 words)
   - **Karst Plateau Origins:** Harsh, rocky terrain, sparse vegetation
   - **Evolution:** Breed developed for survival on minimal resources
   - **Modern Implication:** Inherited hardiness remains
   - **Feed Efficiency:** Easier keepers than many other baroque breeds
   - **Metabolism:** Efficient nutrient utilization
   - **Comparative Advantage:** Lipizzaner cost ~20-30% less to feed than some other noble breeds
   - **Implication for buyers:** Lower annual care costs

2. **Fütterung: Einfach, hochwertig und wirtschaftlich** (95 words)
   - **Base Diet:** High-quality hay (timothy, alfalfa mix, herb hay)
     - Daily: 1.5-2% body weight in hay (8-10 kg for 500 kg horse)
     - Cost: ~€80-€120/month

   - **Grain/Concentrates:**
     - Light work: Minimal grain, mostly forage
     - Moderate work: €0.5-€1.0/day grain
     - Performance: €1.5-€2.5/day grain

   - **Supplements:** Good quality forage typically sufficient
     - Exception: Performance horses (add electrolytes, joint support)

   - **Total monthly feed cost:** €100-€200 (vs €150-€300 for some other breeds)

   - **Fresh water:** Essential, always available

   - **Pasture:** Beneficial; Lipizzaner thrive on pasture (keep slow grazing to prevent obesity)

3. **Haltungsoptionen und Bewegungsfreiheit** (100 words)
   - **Preference:** Strong need for movement
     - Reason: Piber tradition (summer mountain training in pasture)
     - Breeding selected for movement enthusiasts

   - **Offenstall (IDEAL):**
     - 24/7 pasture access with sheltered area
     - Horses self-regulate movement
     - Better mental health, less vices
   - **Box with Turnout (ACCEPTABLE):**
     - Minimum 2-3 hours daily turnout
     - More control, higher maintenance
   - **Pasture Considerations:**
     - Rich pasture: Lipizzaner can over-graze (slow-grazing area recommended)
     - Weather hardy: Thick winter coat adapts well
     - Shelter: Essential (sun, wind, rain protection)
