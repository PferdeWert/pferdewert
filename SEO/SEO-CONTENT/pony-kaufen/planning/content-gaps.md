# Content Gaps Analysis: "pony kaufen" (6,600 monthly searches)

**Analysis Date**: 2025-11-30
**Primary Keyword**: pony kaufen
**Search Intent**: Commercial (84%)
**SERP Snapshot**: Marketplace-dominated (Kleinanzeigen #1, ehorses #2)

---

## Executive Summary

The "pony kaufen" SERP is transactional-heavy (84% commercial intent) with marketplace dominance. However, significant content gaps exist for buyers seeking education before purchase:

- **Zero competition** on AKU (veterinary exam) guidance
- **Zero competition** on red flags/buyer protection
- **Zero competition** on purchase channels comparison
- **Strong competition** on pricing & breed information
- **Weak coverage** on adult rider suitability
- **Weak coverage** on total cost of ownership

**Ranking Strategy**: Position as comprehensive buyer's guide that complements marketplace platforms, not competes with them.

## Completely Missing Topics (Coverage 0-2/10)

These are high-value topics with ZERO competition in Top 10 - first-mover advantage.

### 1. Private Anbieter vs. Züchter vs. Makler - Kaufwege
- **Coverage**: 0/10 (No Top 10 article compares purchasing channels)
- **Current Market**: 5 marketplaces rank (Kleinanzeigen, ehorses, pferde.de, deine-tierwelt, Ponyforum)
- **Opportunity**: Create decision guide:
  - **Marktplätze** (Kleinanzeigen, eBay, pferde.de, ehorses):
    - Pros: Large selection, price comparison, buyer protection (sometimes)
    - Cons: Private sellers mixed with unserialized; scams possible
    - Best for: Budget buyers, browsing wide selection
    - Safety: Always get AKU, meet in person, verify ownership documents
  - **Züchter** (Direct breeder):
    - Pros: Know pony background, genetics, health history
    - Cons: Limited selection, often more expensive, but more reliable
    - Best for: Long-term investment, specific breed/bloodline
    - Safety: Excellent - breeders reputation-dependent
  - **Pferdehändler** (Horse traders):
    - Pros: Professional, often have multiple options, negotiation possible
    - Cons: Higher markups, need to vet dealer reputation
    - Best for: Buyers wanting professional guidance
    - Safety: Moderate - vet the dealer
  - **Facebook Gruppen** (Community groups):
    - Pros: Community vetting, local connections
    - Cons: No buyer protection, variable quality
    - Best for: Local buyers, community recommendations
    - Safety: Depends on group moderation, always get AKU
- **Unique Angle**: Only source giving buyer guidance on which channel by buyer profile/needs.

### 2. Rote Flaggen beim Ponykauf erkennen
- **Coverage**: 0/10 (ZERO competitors address buyer protection/red flags)
- **Opportunity**: Create comprehensive guide:
  - Price red flags: "Too good to be true" pricing (€200 for trained pony = scam)
  - Seller red flags: Refuse AKU, won't show vet records, pressure to buy quickly
  - Pony red flags: Lameness, behavioral issues (biting, rearing), coughing, weight loss
  - Documentation red flags: Missing papers, unclear ownership, forged documents
  - Scenario examples: "Ginger was listed as 'trained' but couldn't be caught. Red flag: Seller avoided demonstrating pony"
  - Checklist: "20-point inspection before final offer"
- **Business Model Link**: "Use pferdewert's AKU-verified pony database to avoid bad purchases"
- **Authority**: Frame as consumer protection guide; drives trust and brand positioning.

### 3. Pony-Lebenserwartung und Langzeitplanung
- **Coverage**: 1/10 (Only in PAA expansion: "30-35 years for Shetland")
- **Opportunity**: Create long-term ownership guide:
  - Average lifespan by breed (Shetland 30-35 vs. Other ponies 25-30)
  - Factors affecting longevity (care, genetics, exercise)
  - Financial planning over 30-year lifespan
  - Health challenges by age (teeth, arthritis, metabolic)
  - Retirement planning (pasture, senior care, euthanasia decisions)
  - Succession planning (what happens when owner grows out?)
- **Unique Angle**: First market source on pony lifespan planning.
